About the Community

Elmont — Nassau's High-Demand Affordable Gem

Elmont occupies a unique and coveted position within Nassau County's real estate hierarchy. It sits directly adjacent to the Queens border — specifically Cambria Heights and Laurelton — and shares the westernmost strip of Nassau with Valley Stream and Franklin Square. Yet despite its gateway location, Elmont has maintained a character and community identity that is distinctly its own, shaped by decades of Caribbean-American settlement and a deep civic pride that runs through its schools, houses of worship, and neighborhood associations.

The housing market here is among the most competitive in western Nassau County. Inventory is chronically low relative to demand, and well-priced homes — particularly well-maintained Cape Cods and colonials — routinely attract multiple offers within 48 to 72 hours of listing. Buyers who are not pre-approved, not working with a well-connected agent, and not prepared to act decisively consistently lose to better-prepared competitors. Nitin Gadura's proximity to this market — his brokerage operates from Ozone Park, less than 10 minutes from Elmont — gives his clients an informational and relational edge that out-of-area agents cannot replicate.

For sellers, Elmont's sustained demand creates exceptional opportunities. Homes that are properly staged, priced to the specific micro-pocket, and marketed with professional photography and MLS exposure regularly achieve sale prices 5–10% above initial list price in the current environment. Nitin's track record in this zip code reflects both the strength of the market and the importance of strategic pricing and presentation.

The community's cultural character is central to its appeal. Elmont's Caribbean-American community — with deep roots stretching back to the mid-20th century immigration waves from Jamaica, Haiti, Trinidad, and Barbados — has created a tight-knit neighborhood fabric with strong schools of culture, active block associations, and a business corridor along Dutch Broadway that serves the community's diverse tastes. More recently, African and South Asian families have added to the community's diversity, reflecting the broader demographic evolution of western Nassau County.

Elmont's proximity to UBS Arena — the state-of-the-art facility that opened in 2021 as the home of the New York Islanders — and to the historic Belmont Park racetrack adds a dimension of civic energy and economic activity to the community. The redevelopment of the Belmont Park site into a mixed-use entertainment and hospitality destination will further elevate Elmont's profile in the coming years, making current buyers potentially well-positioned for long-term appreciation.

Transit in Elmont relies primarily on bus connections to Valley Stream or Jamaica stations, as the community does not have its own LIRR stop. However, the bus network is reliable, and many Elmont residents drive to Valley Stream station for peak-hour train service into Penn Station.

Median sale prices for single-family homes in Elmont (11003) range from approximately $420,000 to $660,000 as of 2024–2025. Elmont is one of the more affordable communities in Nassau County relative to its quality of life and proximity to New York City. Well-maintained colonials and recently renovated Cape Cods can exceed this range depending on location and condition.
Elmont is one of the most competitive markets in western Nassau County. Well-priced homes frequently receive multiple offers within the first week of listing — often selling 5–10% above asking price. Average days on market for well-positioned listings is consistently under 25 days. Buyers must be pre-approved, decisive, and working with an agent who has current market intelligence.
Elmont does not have its own LIRR station. Residents typically drive to Valley Stream station (approximately 5 minutes) for Far Rockaway or Long Beach branch service to Penn Station, or to Jamaica station (approximately 10 minutes) for faster express service. Jamaica also provides access to AirTrain JFK. Bus service connects Elmont to both stations.
Elmont is served by the Elmont Union Free School District, which includes several elementary schools and Elmont Memorial Junior-Senior High School. The district has been on a positive trajectory and is supplemented by private and Catholic school options. Some families near the Valley Stream border may fall within a different district — address verification with the school district is always recommended.
Elmont is known for its vibrant Caribbean-American community, its proximity to UBS Arena (home of the New York Islanders, opened 2021), and its location adjacent to the historic Belmont Park racetrack. The Belmont Park site is undergoing a major mixed-use redevelopment that will add entertainment, hospitality, and retail destinations to the area, enhancing the community's long-term real estate outlook.
