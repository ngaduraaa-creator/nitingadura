Buyer & Seller Guide

Buying & Selling in Queens and Long Island

Everything you need to know about the New York real estate market — from pricing your home to closing day.

What is the average home price in Queens, NY in 2025?

Queens home prices vary significantly by neighborhood. As of 2025, single-family homes in areas like Howard Beach and Forest Hills often exceed $900K, while multi-family properties in South Ozone Park and Richmond Hill range from $700K–$1.1M. Nitin provides precise neighborhood-level pricing based on current OneKey MLS data.

How long does it take to sell a house in Queens?

Well-priced homes in Queens typically sell within 30–60 days. Nitin's listings average significantly less than market time because of strategic pricing, professional photography, and multi-platform syndication on day one. Overpriced homes can sit 90–120 days and often sell for less than market value.

What are closing costs for sellers in New York State?

New York sellers typically pay: agent commission (negotiable), transfer taxes (NYC and NYS), attorney fees, outstanding liens, and any agreed-upon concessions. Nitin provides a complete net sheet before listing so you know exactly what you'll walk away with — no surprises at the closing table.

Is now a good time to sell in Queens or Long Island?

Inventory remains historically low across Queens and Nassau County, which continues to favor sellers. Interest rate fluctuations have moderated buyer demand, but motivated, pre-approved buyers are active. Nitin analyzes current absorption rates by neighborhood before every listing conversation.

What should I do before listing my home in Queens?

Before listing, focus on decluttering, fresh paint in neutral tones, minor repairs, and deep cleaning. Nitin walks every seller through a pre-listing checklist and connects you with trusted contractors for cost-effective improvements that maximize your sale price without over-investing.

How do I buy a home in Queens as a first-time buyer?

Start with mortgage pre-approval to understand your budget. Then work with a buyer's agent (like Nitin) who can identify off-market opportunities, guide you through inspections, and negotiate effectively in a competitive multiple-offer environment. Nitin helps first-time buyers navigate every step at no cost to the buyer.

What is the OneKey MLS and how does it help sellers?

OneKey MLS is the primary Multiple Listing Service for Queens, Nassau, and Suffolk County — covering over 40,000 licensed agents. Listing on OneKey MLS ensures your home is automatically syndicated to Zillow, Realtor.com, Homes.com, StreetEasy, and hundreds of partner sites, reaching the maximum number of qualified buyers.

Do I need an attorney to buy or sell a home in New York?

Yes — New York is an attorney-closing state. Both buyers and sellers are represented by separate real estate attorneys who review contracts, handle title, and oversee the closing. Nitin works closely with experienced Queens and Long Island real estate attorneys and can provide referrals at no extra cost.

What is a comparative market analysis (CMA)?

A CMA (Comparative Market Analysis) is a detailed report of recent comparable sales in your neighborhood used to determine your home's current market value. Nitin provides free, no-obligation CMAs for any Queens, Brooklyn, or Long Island property — delivered within 24 hours of your request.

How is property tax calculated in Queens and Nassau County?

Property taxes in Queens are based on the NYC Department of Finance assessed value, which is often significantly below market value for 1–3 family homes. Nassau County reassesses annually and has higher effective tax rates than many NYC boroughs. Nitin explains the tax implications of every home before you make an offer.

What is the difference between a single-family and multi-family home?

A single-family home is designed for one household. A multi-family home (2–4 units) allows you to live in one unit and rent out others — a popular strategy in Queens and Long Island for building equity while offsetting your mortgage. Nitin specializes in both, with extensive experience in 2- and 3-family properties.

Nitin's listings include professional photography, video walkthroughs, targeted digital advertising, multilingual outreach to Hindi, Punjabi, Gujarati, and Urdu-speaking buyer networks, and full MLS exposure from day one. Most importantly, Nitin is available 7 days a week and responds to every inquiry personally.
