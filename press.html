Expert Quote Bank — Pre-Approved for Verbatim Media Use

On NYC seller closing costs in 2026 "NYC sellers consistently underestimate their closing costs. After commission, NY State transfer tax, NYC RPTT, attorney fees, and any co-op flip tax, total seller-side costs run 8 to 10 percent of sale price. The smart move is requesting a transparent net-sheet before listing — not at closing."
On NYC tenant screening compliance "Source-of-income discrimination is illegal in NY State and NYC. Section 8, CityFHEPS, SSDI, and child-support income must all be considered equally. Most landlord violations come from misunderstanding the rule, not malice — but the fines are the same either way."
On multilingual real estate representation "When parents are funding a $200,000 down payment but the contract is in English they don't speak fluently, having an agent who can run the Hindi or Punjabi conversation directly is the difference between the deal closing and the family walking. We see it every week in Queens."
On 2024 NAR settlement impact "Mandatory written buyer-broker agreements have actually clarified the buyer-side market. Buyers now negotiate commission upfront and understand exactly what they're paying for. The market has shifted toward transparent, performance-based representation — which is healthy for everyone."
On Queens vs Long Island for first-time buyers "A house-hacked Queens 2-family at $800K with FHA at 3.5% down is a more financially viable path to homeownership than a $700K Long Island single-family with 1.8% property taxes. The rental income from the second unit covers most of the mortgage. The math favors Queens for owner-occupants."
