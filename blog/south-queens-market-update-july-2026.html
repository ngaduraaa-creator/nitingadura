Queens Home Sales Dropped 32%, Then Rebounded — Full South Queens Update

South Queens market update · Ozone Park, Woodhaven & Richmond Hill. By Nitin Gadura, NYS Salesperson #10401383405. Published July 13, 2026 · Updated July 17, 2026.

Update, July 17: the winter slowdown described below has reversed. Spring closings (Apr 2–Jun 30) came in at $815,000 median — up 5.0% — on 163 sales, up 4.5% from the prior 90-day window. Ozone Park (11416) led the rebound at a $950,000 median. The original finding is below for context: recorded sales across Ozone Park, Woodhaven, and Richmond Hill (zips 11416–11420) fell 32% quarter-over-quarter over the winter, while price held nearly flat — a pattern that pointed to a slow season rather than a falling market, which the spring data has now confirmed.

Why a 32% drop doesn't mean the market is cooling

Because DOF records closings, not contract signings, that quarter's numbers actually reflected deals made roughly November 2025 through February 2026 — the slowest window of the year for real estate activity almost everywhere in the Northeast. Most of that drop was ordinary seasonality, not a demand shock — and the spring numbers below confirm it.

Ozone Park (11416) led the rebound with a $950,000 median, pulled up by several two- and three-family sales above $1.4 million — multi-family product in this corridor continues to command a real premium. Richmond Hill held closer to flat, still solidly in the $780,000–$800,000 range. Queens borough-wide, June prices were up 3.5% year-over-year, one of the few boroughs posting a gain in an otherwise flat citywide market — our corridor is tracking right in step with that.

One thing still worth watching

Queens remained NYC's most active foreclosure market in Q2 2026, with first-time filings up 9% year-over-year (128 to 140 citywide). Our core zips still aren't among the named hotspots (St. Albans and Jamaica lead), but it's worth continuing to watch for the wider South/Southeast Queens submarket. If you're carrying a property and worried about falling behind, reaching out early gives you far more options — a traditional sale almost always nets more than a distressed one.

What this means if you're thinking about selling

The winter slowdown is confirmed seasonal, not a sign of cooling demand — prices are up 5% and volume has recovered. Mortgage rates did tick up this week to 6.55% (a 10-month high), but that hasn't slowed local buyers, and it's still below where rates sat a year ago. If you've been waiting for a "better" moment to list in this corridor, the data suggests that moment is now, not later.
